History Summary

Established in 1997, Tamale Islamic Senior High School (TISSEC) was founded to as a STEM school by the Islamic Development Bank and used as a temporary lecture space for students of the University for Development Studies. Later, it was absorbed into the Ghanaian public school system.

Tamale Islamic Science Senior High School (TISSEC) was established in 2005 by a coalition of Islamic educators, community leaders, and the Northern Region Muslim community. The school was founded in response to growing demand for quality senior high education that integrates Islamic moral teachings with the national science-oriented curriculum prescribed by the Ghana Education Service (GES).

Starting with a modest number of students and temporary facilities, TISSEC quickly gained recognition for its disciplined environment, strong academic performance, and emphasis on character formation. Over the years, the school has expanded its infrastructure—adding science laboratories, a mosque, dormitories, and a modern classroom block—with support from the Ministry of Education, Muslim charities, and alumni contributions.

As a public/government-assisted school, TISSEC operates under the oversight of the Ghana Education Service while maintaining its unique identity rooted in Islamic values. It is one of the few government-supported Islamic Senior High Schools in Northern Ghana, and it continues to serve students from diverse backgrounds across the region and beyond.

Admission

As a government senior high school, Tamale Islamic Science SHS admits students through the Computerized School Selection and Placement System (CSSPS), managed by the Ghana Education Service (GES).

Students are placed based on their BECE results, school choices, and available spaces. Parents and guardians can confirm their ward's admission status using the official CSSPS placement checker:
